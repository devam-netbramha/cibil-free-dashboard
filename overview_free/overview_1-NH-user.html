Cibil Score

This section reflects your CIBIL Score, which is widely used by loan providers to evaluate loan applications. Your score ranges between 300 and 900, and is calculated based on the information available in th “Accounts” and “Enquiry” section of your CIBIL Report. The closure your score is to 900, the more confidence the lender will have in your ability to repay the loan. Higher your score, the better chances of your application getting approved.

Where is my Cibil Score?

You don not have a CIBIL Score because of any of the following reasons:

  • You have a credit card or loan account, but no credit activity in the last three years.
  • Lenders may have made enquiries, but you do not have any credit activity.
  • You only have add-on credit cards, and no credit exposure.

CIBIL SCORE HISTORY

Instead of the earlier two-year period, your CIBIL Score now factors in a three-year period when evaluating your credit behavior. Assessment of credit data over a longer period helps provide a more comprehensive overview of your loan eligibility.
The enhanced score also includes new credit attributes that gives lenders a more complete view of your credit behavior to help them make objective lending decisions, even if you have a short credit history. The new enhanced score will substitute the current score in all products with a CIBIL Score offering.
